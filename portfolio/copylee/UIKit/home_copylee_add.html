The Lifecycle:

What you don’t know about your participants

Program directors believe they provide what their participants want. You might be surprised at what we found.

To identify your buyers and illustrate their buying decision process, we surveyed over 1,400 parents nationwide on how, when and why they choose activities for their kids.

Here's the inside scoop...

Demography

44% of households surveyed earn between $50 - $125K annually

69% of respondents were female

32% of respondents age 30 to 44

59% of respondents age 45 to 59

Results Overview

$1,100 average annual spend/child on activities

‘Tweens’ (30%) and Teens (37%) are the most active

51% of parents enroll their child in more than 2 activities per year

Discover

During the DISCOVER phase, new participants are trying to find you and existing participants are looking to continue their relationship with you.

What parents say

Only 12% of parents said they find activities through print advertising.

Only 6% of parents stated that they find activities via direct mail.

vs.

What you think they want

52% of program directors said flyers are an important piece of their marketing strategy.

About 30% of program directors listed direct mail as a large marketing channel.

How parents find activities

School

Word of Mouth/Personal Recommendation (including Community Organizations & Events)

Online Search

Social Media

What Should You Do?

  • Survey current participants
  • Network with local businesses and influencers
  • Make sure your website is mobile friendly
  • Improve Your SEO

Engage

During the ENGAGE phase, participants evaluate your product offerings, cost and overall value. Most importantly, they must transact to move to the next phase.

What parents say

87% of parents indicate child's interest is the most important element

vs.

What you think they want

63% of business owners believe price is the biggest motivator

Why Parents Enroll Their Kids

  1. Character and value development
  2. Keeps kids active
  3. Skill development
  4. Social interaction
  5. Passion/competition

What Should You Do?

  • Make the participants the heroes
  • Education before promotion
  • Tell marketing stories with contrast - 'before & after', mix data & emotion
  • Be clear on costs - but don't lead with it

Prepare

During the PREPARE phase, a two-fold opportunity exists: capitalizing on participants’ feelings of anticipation and preparing them for the fun ahead.

What parents say

Over 30% of parents say they invite their friends to the same program

Nearly 40% of parents say they do additional research on the program they registered for

Over 50% of parents say they purchase merchandise to prepare their child for their program

vs.

What you think they want

Less than 50% of camp directors indicated they ask participants to invite friends to register

Only 16% of the programs say they provide more information after registration

Only 7% of camp directors encourage the purchase of gear

What Should You Do?

  • Create special offers – e.g. gear discounts, Friends & Family deals
  • Prep with content: social, blogs & email
  • Provide sneak peeks, insights and ‘teasing’
  • Use social media to create anticipation

Experience

During the EXPERIENCE phase, participants are immersed in your programs – some for the first time – and have direct contact with your staff, facilities, curriculum, and other community members.

69% of parents want program updates and news

53% want photos of their child in the activity

43% of parents want information on other activities

What Should You Do?

  • Make sure every kid is connected
  • Empower staff to know the customer
  • Provide real-time results/feedback
  • Share photos, create social groups
  • Set the stage for reflection

Reflect

During the REFLECT phase, participants are evaluating their overall experience and forming conclusions about whether or not to engage or buy again. Just as important, new prospects will use their feedback in the DISCOVER phase.

User Generated Content (UGC)

84% of buyer decisions are influenced by UGC

35% more memorable than sponsored content

7 in 10 pople share opinions because they believe other consumers care about what they think

90% of participants read peer reviews before purchase

What Should You Do?

  • Conduct surveys and focus groups
  • Suggest complimentary programs
  • Ask for (and monitor) reviews
  • Offer customer loyalty rewards
  • Incentivize social sharing
